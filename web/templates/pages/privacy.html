What we keep, what we don't.

Updated 2026-06-12

In short

We need to know who you are well enough to keep your streak, attribute your decoys to you, and stop the same device from spamming the same prompt. We don't need anything more, and we don't collect anything more.

There are no analytics, no third-party trackers, no advertising IDs, no fingerprinting, no microphone, no camera, no location, no social-login SDKs.

Cookies

Two, both first-party, both required for the service to work. Neither requires consent under the ePrivacy directive: both fall under "strictly necessary."

  • bbg_dev_v1 (365 days). An opaque random token plus an HMAC of it. We store its SHA-256 against a row in our database so we can attach your decoys, streaks, and leaderboard rank to "you, on this device." Carries no personal data and never leaves our server.
  • bbg_csrf_v1 (7 days). A short random token. Prevents another website from forging a form post on your behalf. Carries no identity.

What we store on our server

Tied to your device cookie:

  • An anonymous user row. Just an internal ID until you sign in.
  • Your handle, if you set one.
  • Your email, only if you used the magic-link sign-in. We use it to identify "the same person on a different phone." We never share it, and we never email you anything except a one-tap sign-in link when you ask.
  • Your plays: which puzzles you completed, your per-round outcomes, your Bot-Dar.
  • Your streak.
  • Your decoys: the human answers you submitted, attached to the prompt each is for.
  • Your forger rank, derived from how those decoys performed.

For the harvest campaign (the Reddit landing page):

  • Your harvested answers, attached to the prompt each is for.
  • Your IP address, briefly, for spam rate-limiting.

For security:

  • Hashed magic-link tokens, while they're outstanding (15 min) and as a post-consume audit row.
  • Your IP address in rate-limit buckets so we can spot scripted abuse.
  • Your User-Agent string, for debugging access patterns.

What we explicitly don't store

  • Your email is never written into our magic_links table. It's sealed inside the signed sign-in link itself; the row only stores a hash. A database dump alone reveals no addresses.
  • Your passwords: we don't have any. Sign-in is magic-link only.
  • Your location: we never request it.
  • The shared grid carries no spoilers and no personal data.
  • Any analytics events: we have none.

Third parties

  • Resend sends our sign-in emails. They receive your email address (they have to, to deliver) and retain delivery logs as their own policy describes. We use no other Resend feature.
  • Cloudflare may terminate TLS and bridge your traffic to our server. Standard reverse-proxy logging applies on their side.
  • Anthropic generates the bot answers, offline only. Your data is never sent to Anthropic.

That's the whole list. There is no fourth party.

How long we keep things

device_cookies 1 year of inactivity
magic_links 15 min (active) + audit retention
pre_launch_submissions (IP) until the harvest campaign closes
plays, decoys, leaderboards until you ask us to delete

We don't automatically purge yet: a cleanup job is on the backlog. In the meantime, request deletion any time and we will action it manually.

Identifying yourself

Before we can act on a data-access or deletion request, we need to be confident it's genuinely from the person whose data it concerns. How that works depends on whether you've signed in.

  • Signed in with email. We send a one-tap verification link to your address; click it to confirm. Same flow as signing in.
  • Anonymous only. The bbg_dev_v1 cookie in your browser is the only identity we have for you. The cleanest path is to sign in via magic-link from that device first (this merges your anonymous activity into the resulting account) and then use the signed-in path above.

If you've cleared the cookie, used incognito, or lost the device, we genuinely can't match a name in your message to a row in our database. We don't keep a secondary index that would let us undo anonymity on someone's behalf. GDPR Article 11 allows the controller to decline a request when the data subject can no longer be identified, and that's the position we'd be in. Anonymous play is anonymous, on purpose.

Your rights

Once you've verified yourself (above), you can:

  • Ask what we have on you. File a request via the channel below; we'll send a copy of every row tied to your verified account.
  • Ask us to delete your account. We will delete your user row, your decoys, your plays, your streak, and your forger rank. We'll also unbind anonymous content the same device produced before you signed in.
  • Sign out of every device. The "Sign out everywhere" button on /me does this without involving us. It doesn't delete data, just revokes session access.

When this page changes

If we add a third party, change what we store, or change a retention window, this page updates and we'll note the change in the project CHANGELOG.

Contact

For security-sensitive issues, file a private report via GitHub Security Advisories. For non-security privacy questions or deletion requests, open an issue on the public tracker or reply to any system email you've received from us.
